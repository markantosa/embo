======================================================================
1. SYSTEM OVERVIEW
======================================================================
EMBO is a closed-loop embolic agent preparation device. The MCU board integrates three sensor subsystems (UAS, CV camera, StallGuard viscosity), two stepper motor actuators, two mechanical limit switches, and a touch-screen UI — coordinated by an ESP32-S3 communicating with a Raspberry Pi 5 via UART.

1.1 Why ESP32-S3-WROOM-1-N8
---------------------------
Module integrates chip, 8MB flash, 40MHz crystal, PCB antenna — no external MCU support components needed
Built-in USB Serial/JTAG on GPIO19/20 — program via USB-C, no bridge IC needed
Bluetooth LE 5.0 on-chip — NimBLE library for wireless debug stream
36 GPIO broken out — 25 used, 11 free
N8 variant: no PSRAM — GPIO35–37 free for SPI bus

3. POWER SECTION
======================================================================

3.1 Reverse polarity protection — AO4407A P-channel MOSFET
----------------------------------------------------------
Changed from AO3401A (SOT-23) in v2.1 to AO4407A (SOIC-8) for higher current handling. The AO4407A has Vgs_max = ±20V. With a 24V supply and gate pulled directly to GND, Vgs = −24V which exceeds this limit. A 15V Zener diode (D7) clamps the gate-source voltage to a safe −15V.

Component  |  Value / Part                                 |  Connection                                                                                                                                           
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Q1         |  AO4407A SOIC-8 P-channel MOSFET, −30V, −12A  |  Source (pins 2–4) → J14 V+. Drain (pins 5–8) → protected 24V rail. Gate (pin 1) → R_gate / D7 junction.                                              
R_gate     |  10kΩ 0402                                    |  Gate → GND. Ensures MOSFET turns off when input is disconnected.                                                                                     
D7         |  BZX84C15 15V Zener SOT-23                    |  Cathode → Source (24V+ line). Anode → Gate junction. Clamps Vgs to −15V during normal operation. Without D7, Vgs = −24V exceeds AO4407A ±20V Vgs_max.

Operation: normal polarity — Gate is clamped to (24V − 15V) = 9V by D7, giving Vgs = 9 − 24 = −15V. P-channel turns ON. Reverse polarity — Source becomes negative, Gate is pulled to circuit GND (now the positive rail), Vgs becomes positive, P-channel stays OFF. Circuit protected.

5. UAS SIGNAL CHAIN
======================================================================
1MHz acoustic signal chain for particle attenuation measurement. All on 3.3V rail. AD9833 can sweep 0.5–3MHz in firmware for spectral data — same hardware. All gains verified against 3.3V supply rail limits.

5.1 AD9833BRMZ — signal generator
---------------------------------

Parameter        |  Requirement                                                                             
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IC               |  AD9833BRMZ (MSOP-10)                                                                    
Supply           |  3.3V — both VDD pins                                                                    
SPI              |  GPIO38 (FSYNC), GPIO36 (SCLK), GPIO35 (SDATA) — write-only. SPI Mode 2 (CPOL=1, CPHA=0).
RSET             |  6.8kΩ 1% 0402 to AGND → IOUT ≈ 3.02mA → 604mVpp across 200Ω load                        
COMP             |  10nF X7R 0402 to AGND                                                                   
Output coupling  |  100nF X7R 0402 from IOUT load to Tx amp input                                           
Decoupling       |  100nF X7R per VDD pin + 10µF X5R 0805 bulk                                              


5.2 Tx amplifier — IC1 channel A, G = 4.9
-----------------------------------------

Parameter       |  Requirement                                                                  
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IC              |  OPA2354 SOIC-8, channel A of IC1. 250MHz GBW. 3.3V single supply.            
Bias            |  Two 10kΩ (3.3V → non-inv → GND) — 1.65V mid-rail                             
Input coupling  |  100nF X7R from AD9833                                                        
Gain            |  Rf = 390Ω 1%, Rg = 100Ω 1%. G = 4.9. Output = 2.96Vpp. Peak = 3.13V < 3.3V. ✓
Output          |  47Ω series + 100nF coupling to J1 SMA                                        


5.3 IC1 channel B — unused, must be tied off
--------------------------------------------
Pin 5 (non-inv input ch-B): connect to 1.65V mid-rail node from existing 10kΩ/10kΩ divider
Pin 6 (inv input ch-B): connect directly to pin 7 (output ch-B) — unity-gain follower. Prevents unused channel oscillating.

5.4 Rx input protection — J2 SMA → R_in → D1 BAV99
--------------------------------------------------

Component  |  Value            |  Function                                
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
J2         |  SMA edge-launch  |  Rx transducer input                     
R_in       |  100Ω 0402        |  Series protection — limits fault current
D1         |  BAV99 SOT-23     |  Dual diode clamp to 0V and 3.3V         


5.5 Rx two-stage amplification — IC2, total G = 100 (+40 dB)
------------------------------------------------------------

Stage       |  IC             |  Rf       |  Rg       |  Gain      |  Notes                                           
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rx stage 1  |  IC2 channel A  |  910Ω 1%  |  100Ω 1%  |  G = 10.1  |  BW = 25MHz >> 1MHz ✓                            
Rx stage 2  |  IC2 channel B  |  910Ω 1%  |  100Ω 1%  |  G = 10.1  |  Total: 100× — max non-clipping Rx input = 32mVpp

Both stages have 10kΩ/10kΩ mid-rail bias dividers and 100nF AC coupling at input and between stages. IC2 V+ decoupling: 100nF + 10µF bulk. If received signal is too weak in lab, replace Rf 910Ω with 3.9kΩ in IC2 ch-A only for G=40 — single resistor swap, no PCB change.

5.6 Envelope detector and ADC input
-----------------------------------

Component  |  Value           |  Function                                                                  
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
D2         |  BAT54 SOD-123   |  Envelope rectifier, Vf ≈ 0.2V. Anode: IC2 ch-B output. Cathode: RC filter.
R_env      |  10kΩ 0402       |  RC discharge: τ = 100µs, fc = 1.6kHz, ripple at 1MHz = 0.16%              
C_env      |  10nF X7R 0402   |  RC filter in parallel with R_env. Settling time (5τ) = 500µs.             
D3         |  BAV99 SOT-23    |  Hard clamp at GPIO1 — overvoltage protection                              
C_adc      |  100nF X7R 0402  |  GPIO1 to AGND noise filter                                                

No voltage divider at GPIO1. Max envelope ≈ 1.4V. ESP32 ADC in 11dB attenuation mode accepts 0–3.1V — 1.4V fits with 2.2× headroom. ADC resolution: 0.757mV/LSB. Firmware: configure ADC1_CH0 in ADC_ATTEN_DB_11.

6. MOTOR DRIVERS — 2× TMC2209-LA
======================================================================
Two TMC2209-LA QFN-28 ICs drive two NEMA17 stepper motors. Shared half-duplex UART on GPIO4. StallGuard4 provides motor-load viscosity proxy via SG_RESULT in DRVSTATUS register. Hot plate reflow with solder paste and stencil resolves the QFN hand-soldering concern.

6.1 UART addressing
-------------------

Driver   |  Ref  |  MS1               |  MS2               |  UART address
--------------------------------------------------------------------------
Motor 1  |  U5   |  GND (tie direct)  |  0               
Motor 2  |  U6   |  3.3V via 10kΩ     |  GND (tie direct)  |  1           

1kΩ series resistor between GPIO4 and UART junction. Both PDN_UART pins connect to junction. UART1 TX and RX both mapped to GPIO4 via GPIO matrix in firmware. Enable SpreadCycle via UART GCONF register at startup — StallGuard inactive in default StealthChop mode.

6.2 Power and critical pins — each TMC2209
------------------------------------------

Pin          |  Connection                  |  Notes                                                                                                           
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
VM           |  24V motor rail              |  100µF electrolytic + 100nF X7R at each IC VM pin — separate, not shared                                         
VCC_IO       |  3.3V                        |  Logic supply                                                                                                    
VCP          |  100nF X7R 0402 to GND       |  CRITICAL — charge pump for high-side gate driver. Without this cap the motor will not run. Within 2mm.          
SPREAD       |  3.3V via 10kΩ               |  Forces SpreadCycle in hardware — StallGuard requires SpreadCycle. Failsafe if firmware fails to configure GCONF.
EN           |  10kΩ pull-up to 3.3V at IC  |  Active LOW. Pull-up overrides GPIO7/10 power-up glitch.                                                         
CLK          |  Floating (DNP pad to GND)   |  Enables internal 12MHz oscillator                                                                               
VREF         |  Floating (DNP test point)   |  Self-biases to VCC_IO/2 = 1.65V. Current via UART IRUN/IHOLD.                                                   
Exposed pad  |  GND pour via ≥4 vias        |  Thermal path — mandatory connection to GND

10. PCB LAYOUT RULES
======================================================================

10.1 Layer stack
----------------
2-layer FR4, 1oz copper, ENIG. Top: all SMD + signal routing. Bottom: ground plane + power routing.
UAS analog signals on top layer only — no vias in UAS signal paths.

10.2 Antenna keepout
--------------------
No copper, traces, vias, or components within 15mm of ESP32 PCB antenna on ALL layers.
Place module at board edge with antenna pointing outward.

10.3 Power section
------------------
Q1 AO4407A: D7 Zener (BZX84C15) cathode to Source side, anode to Gate. R_gate 10kΩ from Gate to GND. Place D7 within 3mm of Q1.
SS34A catch diode within 5mm of LM2596 SW pin. 10Ω + 1nF snubber from SW pin to GND.
AMS1117 SOT-223 tab: ≥100mm² top copper + ≥4 thermal vias to bottom ground plane.

10.4 UAS signal chain
---------------------
IC1 (Tx) and IC2 (Rx): ≥15mm physical separation.
J2 → R_in → D1 → IC2 ch-A input: under 20mm, no vias, ground guard traces.
Envelope RC within 5mm of IC2 ch-B output. GPIO1 trace under 10mm, no vias.

10.5 TMC2209 motor drivers
--------------------------
QFN-28: use solder paste + stencil + hot plate reflow. Verify GND continuity on thermal pad after reflow.
VCP cap (100nF) within 2mm of VCP pin of each IC — critical, separate per IC.
VM decoupling (100µF + 100nF) directly at each IC VM pin — separate per IC.
Motor coil traces (J4/J5 outputs): ≥0.5mm width. Current sense resistors: Kelvin connections.
Motor connectors J4 and J5: mark pin 1 position on silkscreen. NC pads on pins 5–6 should be connected to GND or left as no-connect — do not leave floating copper.
